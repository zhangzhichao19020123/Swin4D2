Block-wise Variable Conditioanl Diffusion for High-Fidelity Text-to-4D Generation

Abstract

Text-to-4D scene generation is currently impeded by three primary challenges: the prohibitive memory cost of training high-dimensional diffusion models, the semantic misalignment between concise text prompts and complex dynamic scenes, and the scarcity of high-quality datasets that accurately correlate subjects, motions, and environments. To surmount these challenges, we present \textbf{Swin4D}, an end-to-end framework designed for efficient and high-fidelity text-to-4D synthesis. First, we introduce a \textit{Block-wise Variable Conditional Diffusion} strategy. This approach mitigates memory constraints and prevents overfitting by dynamically reconfiguring spatial dependencies during training, thereby exposing the model to diverse condition-target permutations. Second, we propose \textit{ Global-Local Alignment}, a hierarchical guidance mechanism that fuses global scene context with local subject-action details. By adaptively modulating these features, our method ensures both spatiotemporal consistency and fine-grained semantic fidelity. Finally, we address data limitations by curating a large-scale hybrid dataset combining real-world captures with AI-synthesized scenes. Unlike existing datasets that often feature simplistic solid-color backgrounds, our dataset establishes robust correlations between subjects, actions, and complex backgrounds, significantly enhancing model generalization.

Method

Swin4D Training Pipeline. It transforms text prompts into dynamic 4D scenes through VAE encoding, dual-level text alignment (global and local), guided diffusion transformers with block-wise processing, and 4D Gaussian Splatting to generate multiview videos.
